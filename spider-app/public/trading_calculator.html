Old School vs Modern School of Trading
A comprehensive comparison of classical and contemporary trading philosophies, tools, and methodology
🏛️ Old School Classical Methods
1900s – 1990s | Foundation Pillars of Technical Analysis

Born from the minds of legendary traders — Dow, Wyckoff, Gann, and Livermore. Old school methods are pure, structural, and deeply psychological. They require patience, reading the tape, understanding accumulation/distribution, and respecting natural market cycles. These principles hold today because human psychology hasn't changed.

🚀 Modern School of Trading
2000s – Present | Data-Driven, Algorithmic, and Institutional

Modern trading evolved with computing power, high-frequency systems, and global market connectivity. Smart Money Concepts (SMC), Order Flow, and algorithmic execution represent the new language of markets. The best modern traders blend quantitative rigor with price-action insight, operating at machine speed with institutional precision.
